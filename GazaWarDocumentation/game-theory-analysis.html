Multi-Player Nash Equilibrium Analysis

Multi-Player Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium

Equilibrium Condition for n-Player Game:

$$\forall i \in P: \max_{s_i} U_i(s_i, s_{-i}^*, \theta_i) \quad \text{where } s_{-i}^* \text{ are equilibrium strategies of all other players}$$

Coalition Stability Condition:

$$\forall C \subseteq P: \sum_{i \in C} U_i(s_C', s_{-C}^*) \leq \sum_{i \in C} U_i(s_C^*, s_{-C}^*)$$

Information Structure:

$$I_i = \{H_i, \Theta_{-i}, S_{observable}\} \quad \text{where } H_i \text{ is player i's history}$$

  • Private information about domestic constraints prevents coordination
  • Media filtering creates different information sets for different players
  • Intelligence sharing limited to alliance partners
Commitment Problems:
  • No credible third-party enforcement mechanism
  • Time-inconsistent preferences across electoral cycles
  • Coalition instability prevents long-term commitments

Coalition Formation Analysis

Core Coalition Stability:

$$Core(N, v) = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n : \sum_{i \in N} x_i = v(N) \land \forall S \subseteq N, \sum_{i \in S} x_i \geq v(S)\}$$

Shapley Value for Coalition Payoffs:

$$\phi_i(N, v) = \sum_{S \subseteq N \setminus \{i\}} \frac{|S|!(n-|S|-1)!}{n!}[v(S \cup \{i\}) - v(S)]$$

Mechanism Design for Peace

Incentive Compatible Peace Mechanism:

$$IC: \forall i, \forall \theta_i, \theta_i' : U_i(g(\theta_i, \theta_{-i}), \theta_i) \geq U_i(g(\theta_i', \theta_{-i}), \theta_i)$$

Individual Rationality Constraint:

$$IR: \forall i, \forall \theta_i : U_i(g(\theta_i, \theta_{-i}), \theta_i) \geq U_i^{status\ quo}$$

Budget Balance:

$$BB: \sum_{i=1}^n t_i(\theta) \geq 0 \quad \text{where } t_i \text{ are transfer payments}$$

Policy Implications

Incentive Misalignment

Current incentive structures reward conflict continuation over peace-seeking behavior

$$Incentive_{Peace} < Incentive_{Conflict}$$

Structural Solutions

Breaking the equilibrium requires changing the payoff structure:

  • International accountability mechanisms
  • Conditional aid structures
  • Electoral reform incentives
  • Third-party enforcement

Time Inconsistency Problem

$$U_{t=0}(Peace) > U_{t=0}(War)$$ $$U_{t=election}(War) > U_{t=election}(Peace)$$

Short-term political incentives override long-term social welfare
